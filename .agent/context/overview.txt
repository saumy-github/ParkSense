COMPETITION OVERVIEW
====================
Flipkart Gridlock Hackathon 2.0 — Round 2 (Prototype Phase)
Hosted on HackerEarth | Organised by Flipkart × Bengaluru Traffic Police (ASTraM)

---

PROBLEM DESCRIPTION
-------------------
Theme 1 (selected): Poor Visibility on Parking-Induced Congestion

Operational challenge:
  On-street illegal parking and spillover parking near commercial areas, metro
  stations, and events choke carriageways and intersections. Enforcement is
  patrol-based and reactive. There is no heatmap of parking violations vs.
  congestion impact, and it is difficult to prioritise enforcement zones.

Problem statement direction:
  How can AI-driven parking intelligence detect illegal parking hotspots and
  quantify their impact on traffic flow to enable targeted enforcement?

Broader context:
  Bengaluru (14M+ population) loses billions of productive hours annually to
  chronic traffic congestion. CCTV cameras are deployed city-wide by the
  Bengaluru Traffic Police but most analysis is still manual, limiting speed,
  accuracy, and scale. The hackathon asks participants to design AI solutions
  that classify congestion, detect violations, identify movement patterns, and
  support smarter mobility decisions — built on real data, not simulations.

This round (Round 2) is a PROTOTYPE phase. We are building a working prototype,
not just an ML model. The solution must demonstrate real-world viability.

---

EVALUATION CRITERIA (Round 2 — Prototype)
------------------------------------------
Judging is qualitative, assessed by an expert panel including police officials:
  - Solution robustness
  - Innovation
  - Prototype clarity
  - Scalability
  - Real-world viability for Bengaluru's traffic

(Phase 1 leaderboard used R² against hidden labels; Phase 2 shifts to prototype
quality — there is no single numeric metric for this round.)
